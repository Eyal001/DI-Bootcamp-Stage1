Biography



Eli Cohen was an Israeli spy born on December 26, 1924, in Alexandria, Egypt. He emigrated to Israel in 1946 and joined the Israel Defense Forces. In 1961, he was recruited by the Mossad to infiltrate the Syrian government under the alias "Kamel Amin Thaabet." Cohen gained the trust of high-ranking officials, providing crucial intelligence that greatly benefited Israel during the Six-Day War in 1967. Unfortunately, he was captured by Syrian intelligence in 1965. Despite enduring torture, he refused to reveal his true identity. He was executed in Damascus in 1967. Cohen is remembered as a national hero and a symbol of bravery and sacrifice, with his story continuing to inspire books and films about his life and contributions to Israeli security.
